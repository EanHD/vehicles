Safety Precautions

ALWAYS disconnect negative battery cable first, then positive cable to prevent electrical shorts and potential airbag deployment
Never work on charging system with battery connected - risk of electrical shock and component damage
Wear safety glasses and gloves - battery acid and electrical components present burn and shock hazards
Allow engine to cool completely before beginning work - alternator and surrounding components reach temperatures exceeding 200°F
Do NOT drop tools or metal objects onto battery terminals - can cause explosive sparks and battery damage
Hybrid models contain high-voltage components - only qualified technicians should service hybrid charging systems
Verify serpentine belt is properly seated on all pulleys before starting engine - improper routing can cause belt to jump off and damage components
Keep hands, clothing, and tools away from cooling fan - fan may activate unexpectedly even with engine off

Service Procedure

  1. Disconnect the negative battery cable first using a 10mm socket, then disconnect the positive cable. Push connectors away from battery terminals to prevent accidental contact during service.
    Time: 3 min
  2. Remove the engine cover by pulling upward on the retaining clips. Set aside in a clean area.
    Time: 2 min
  3. Locate the serpentine belt tensioner. For 1.5L turbo engine, use a 19mm wrench or breaker bar on the tensioner bolt. Rotate tensioner clockwise to release belt tension. Slide belt off alternator pulley first, then release tensioner slowly.
    Time: 8 min
  4. Optional but recommended for 1.5L engine: Remove coolant reservoir by disconnecting hose and removing mounting bolts. This provides better access to alternator.
    Time: 5 min
  5. Press the clip on the electrical connector at the alternator and carefully pull to disconnect. Pull back the rubber boot covering the battery cable terminal on alternator.
    Time: 3 min
  6. Remove the 10mm nut securing the battery cable to the alternator. Keep nut in safe location as it is easy to drop. (Torque: 6 ft-lbs)
    Time: 2 min
  7. For 1.5L engine: Remove two 12mm mounting bolts securing alternator to bracket (one top, one bottom). For 2.0L engine: Remove three 12mm mounting bolts. Note that longer bolt goes on top position.
    Time: 5 min
  8. For 2.0L engine only: Remove plastic cooling fan shroud by removing retaining clips and bolts to provide clearance for alternator removal.
    Time: 8 min
  9. Maneuver alternator out of engine bay. Orient pulley downward and two mounting brackets as shown. Lift from underneath A/C line. For 1.5L, alternator removes from top. For 2.0L, may need to use pry bar to break alternator free from brackets if tight fit.
    Time: 10 min
  10. Compare old and new alternators. Verify mounting holes, pulley orientation, and electrical connectors match exactly before installation.
    Time: 3 min
  11. Install new alternator by reversing removal orientation. Slide under A/C hose and position roughly into mounting location.
    Time: 8 min
  12. Connect battery cable to alternator post before installing mounting bolts. Install 10mm nut and torque to specification. (Torque: 6 ft-lbs)
    Time: 3 min
  13. Install alternator mounting bolts. For 1.5L: install two 12mm bolts (longer one on top). For 2.0L: install three 12mm bolts. Start top bolt by hand first, then install remaining bolts. (Torque: 16 ft-lbs)
    Time: 5 min
  14. Connect electrical connector to alternator. Press firmly until it clicks into place. Ensure rubber boot covers battery cable terminal.
    Time: 2 min
  15. For 2.0L engine: Reinstall cooling fan shroud with retaining clips and bolts.
    Time: 5 min
  16. Reinstall serpentine belt following belt routing diagram. Use 19mm wrench on tensioner, rotate to create slack, route belt over all pulleys, ensure proper seating in all pulley grooves.
    Time: 10 min
  17. If removed, reinstall coolant reservoir with mounting bolts and reconnect hose.
    Time: 4 min
  18. Reinstall engine cover by aligning clips and pressing down until secured.
    Time: 2 min
  19. Reconnect battery cables. Connect positive cable first, then negative cable. Tighten with 10mm socket.
    Time: 3 min
  20. Start engine and verify charging system operation. With engine running, measure voltage at battery terminals. Should read 13.5-14.5 volts. Check for proper belt operation and listen for unusual noises.
    Time: 5 min

Common Issues & Troubleshooting

Premature alternator bearing failure on 1.5L turbo engines: Tenth-generation Accords with 1.5L turbo have reported alternator bearing noise between 40,000-70,000 miles. Symptoms include grinding or whining noise that increases with engine speed. Caused by inadequate bearing lubrication in certain production batches. Solution: Replace with updated alternator unit and verify belt tension is within specification to prevent premature wear.
Difficult alternator removal due to tight clearances: On both 1.5L and 2.0L engines, alternator can become wedged between A/C lines and chassis components. Symptoms include alternator appearing stuck even with all bolts removed. Caused by thermal expansion creating tight fit. Solution: Use pry bar carefully at mounting ears (not on housing) to break alternator free. On 2.0L, removing fan shroud provides necessary clearance.
Serpentine belt routing confusion: Multiple accessory configurations make belt routing errors common. Symptoms include belt slipping, squealing, or being loose after installation. Caused by incorrect pulley routing. Solution: Photograph belt routing before removal or reference diagram on radiator support. Belt must wrap around all accessory pulleys in correct sequence with ribbed side contacting all pulleys.
Electrical connector damage during removal: Connector tab on alternator is plastic and can break if forced. Symptoms include loose connection or inability to reconnect. Caused by not pressing release tab fully before pulling. Solution: Press release tab completely while gently wiggling connector side-to-side. If tab breaks, connector must be replaced to ensure proper electrical contact.
Battery drain after installation: New alternator not charging properly after installation. Symptoms include battery warning light on dashboard, low voltage readings, or dead battery after short period. Caused by loose battery cable connection at alternator, improper electrical connector seating, or defective new alternator. Solution: Verify 10mm battery cable nut is torqued to 6 ft-lbs, electrical connector clicks fully into place, and voltage output is 13.5-14.5V with engine running.
Hybrid system alternator confusion: 2.0L Hybrid uses integrated motor-generator unit, not conventional alternator. Symptoms include inability to locate traditional alternator. Caused by different hybrid charging system architecture. Solution: Hybrid models require specialized Honda dealer service for charging system repairs. Do not attempt conventional alternator replacement on hybrid variants.
