Safety Precautions

Never work under a vehicle supported only by a jack; always use jack stands.
Allow engine to cool to avoid burns from hot oil or components.
Wear gloves and eye protection to prevent contact with hot oil and debris.
Dispose of used oil and filter properly according to local regulations.
Ensure vehicle is on a level surface before starting work.

Service Procedure

  1. Warm up the engine for 2-3 minutes, then turn it off and allow to cool until warm to the touch. Open the hood and remove the oil filler cap and dipstick to allow airflow.
    Time: 3 min
  2. Safely raise the vehicle using a jack and secure with jack stands. Remove the underbody splash shield using a #3 Phillips screwdriver and flathead if equipped.
    Time: 5 min
  3. Position an oil drain pan under the oil pan. Use a 17mm socket to remove the oil drain plug. Allow oil to drain for 5-10 minutes.
    Time: 10 min
  4. Inspect the drain plug and crush washer. Replace the washer with a new OEM part. Clean the drain plug threads.
    Time: 2 min
  5. Reinstall the drain plug with new washer. Torque to 30 ft-lbs (Honda factory spec). (Torque: 30 ft-lbs)
    Time: 2 min
  6. Locate and remove the oil filter using an oil filter wrench. Clean the mounting surface. Ensure the old gasket is not stuck to the engine.
    Time: 3 min
  7. Apply a thin film of new oil to the new filter gasket. Install new filter hand-tight, then tighten an additional 3/4 turn. (Torque: Hand-tight + 3/4 turn)
    Time: 2 min
  8. Reinstall the splash shield if removed.
    Time: 3 min
  9. Lower the vehicle. Add specified oil quantity: 1.5L Turbo – 3.7 quarts (3.5L), 2.0L Turbo – 4.4 quarts (4.2L), Hybrid – 4.5 quarts (4.3L). Use 0W-20 full synthetic oil.
    Time: 5 min
  10. Start engine and let run for 1-2 minutes. Shut off, wait 2 minutes, and check oil level on dipstick. Top off as needed to bring level between MIN and MAX marks.
    Time: 4 min
  11. Check for leaks at drain plug and filter. Wipe any spilled oil. Reset oil life monitor via dashboard controls.
    Time: 3 min

Common Issues & Troubleshooting

Oil leaks from drain plug: Caused by worn or reused crush washer. Symptom: Oil drips under car after service. Solution: Always replace washer with new OEM part at each oil change. If threads are damaged, inspect for cross-threading and repair with thread insert if needed.
Stripped drain plug threads: Caused by over-torquing or cross-threading. Symptom: Drain plug will not tighten or leaks persist. Solution: Use correct 17mm socket and torque to 30 ft-lbs. Severe cases may require oil pan replacement or thread repair kit.
Incorrect oil viscosity: Using oil other than 0W-20 can cause poor fuel economy, engine noise, or VTC (Variable Timing Control) issues. Solution: Always verify and use 0W-20 full synthetic as specified in owner's manual.
Oil filter gasket left behind: Old gasket stuck to engine block can cause severe leaks. Symptom: Oil leaks immediately after startup. Solution: Always inspect mounting surface and remove old gasket before installing new filter.
Maintenance minder not reset: Oil life monitor will not update. Symptom: Dashboard continues to show 'Service Due Soon' after oil change. Solution: Reset via steering wheel controls after service[2][3].
