Safety Precautions

Never work under vehicle supported only by hydraulic jack—always use jack stands rated for 2+ tons.
Wear eye protection and nitrile gloves when handling brake components and chemicals.
Brake dust may contain asbestos—avoid blowing with compressed air; use brake cleaner and damp rags.
Do not compress caliper piston rapidly—can damage ABS hydraulic unit.
Ensure brake pedal is firm before moving vehicle—failure to do so may result in loss of braking.
Dispose of used brake pads and fluid at certified recycling centers—do not discard in trash.

Service Procedure

  1. Verify correct front brake pad part number for 2022 Honda Accord (OEM: 45022-TVA-A01 for 1.5T/2.0T, 45022-TVA-A51 for Hybrid). Confirm rotor thickness is above minimum specification: 1.02 in (26 mm) new, 0.98 in (25 mm) minimum. Check brake fluid level (should be clear amber, not dark brown) and inspect brake lines for leaks or damage.
    Time: 5 min
  2. Loosen front wheel lug nuts 1/2 turn with a 19 mm socket before raising vehicle. Do not remove completely.
    Time: 2 min
  3. Raise vehicle using floor jack at manufacturer lift points (pinch welds behind front wheels). Secure with jack stands rated for 2+ tons. Never work under vehicle supported only by jack.
    Time: 5 min
  4. Remove front wheel lug nuts and wheel. Set aside. Inspect tire and wheel for abnormal wear or damage.
    Time: 3 min
  5. Remove caliper mounting bolts (2 x 14 mm, located on caliper slide pins). Use 14 mm socket and ratchet. Factory torque spec for reinstallation: 25 ft-lbs (34 Nm). (Torque: 25 ft-lbs (34 Nm))
    Time: 3 min
  6. Carefully lift caliper off rotor. Support caliper with mechanic's wire or S-hook from suspension. Never let caliper hang by brake hose.
    Time: 2 min
  7. Remove old brake pads and anti-rattle clips from caliper bracket. Note wear pattern for diagnostics. Discard old hardware.
    Time: 2 min
  8. Inspect caliper slide pins for binding or corrosion. Remove pins, clean with brake cleaner, and re-lubricate with high-temp synthetic brake grease (e.g., Sil-Glyde or Permatex Ceramic Extreme). Reinstall pins.
    Time: 4 min
  9. Compress caliper piston slowly using a C-clamp or brake caliper tool. Place old pad against piston to distribute force. Monitor brake fluid reservoir—remove excess fluid if near max to prevent overflow.
    Time: 4 min
  10. Install new anti-rattle clips (included with pad kit) onto caliper bracket. Ensure correct orientation and secure fit.
    Time: 2 min
  11. Install new brake pads (OEM 45022-TVA-A01 or equivalent) into bracket with chamfered edges facing rotor. If equipped, install new pad wear sensor (top of inboard pad, Hybrid models only).
    Time: 3 min
  12. Apply thin coat of high-temp brake grease to pad backing plates and contact points (NOT friction surface).
    Time: 2 min
  13. Reinstall caliper over new pads and rotor. Align slide pins. Install and hand-tighten caliper mounting bolts. Torque to 25 ft-lbs (34 Nm) using torque wrench. (Torque: 25 ft-lbs (34 Nm))
    Time: 3 min
  14. Reinstall wheel. Hand-tighten lug nuts in star pattern. Lower vehicle to ground. Torque lug nuts to 80 ft-lbs (108 Nm) in star pattern using torque wrench. (Torque: 80 ft-lbs (108 Nm))
    Time: 5 min
  15. Pump brake pedal slowly 3-5 times until firm before moving vehicle. Check brake fluid level and top off with Honda DOT 3 or DOT 4 as needed.
    Time: 2 min
  16. Perform bedding-in procedure: 30 gentle stops from 30 mph (48 km/h) with 30-second cool-downs between stops. Avoid hard braking for first 200 miles.
    Time: 10 min
  17. After test drive, recheck all mounting bolts and brake fluid level. Inspect for leaks, abnormal noises, or warning lights.
    Time: 4 min

Platform Variants

Important differences based on platform codes, VIN splits, or production dates.

  • Hybrid models (VIN 1HGCV3...) use different front pad set (45022-TVA-A51) and may have a pad wear sensor (left front only).
  • 1.5T and 2.0T models (VIN 1HGCV1..., 1HGCV2...) use standard pad set (45022-TVA-A01), no wear sensor.
  • Caliper bracket bolts (17 mm, 79 ft-lbs/107 Nm) only need removal if replacing rotors.
  • Rotor minimum thickness: 0.98 in (25 mm) for all variants—replace if below spec.

Common Issues & Troubleshooting

Uneven pad wear: Technician observes one pad significantly thinner than the other. Root cause: seized or dry slide pins, improper lubrication, or caliper binding. Solution: Remove, clean, and lubricate slide pins with high-temp brake grease (OEM 08798-9002). Replace pins if corroded (OEM 45235-TVA-A01, ~$12 each).
Brake squeal after pad change: Squealing noise during light braking, especially at low speed. Root cause: missing or improperly installed anti-rattle clips, lack of grease on pad backing plates, or contaminated rotor. Solution: Reinstall hardware, apply thin coat of brake grease to pad backs, clean rotor with brake cleaner.
Soft brake pedal after service: Pedal sinks or feels spongy. Root cause: Caliper piston not fully seated, air introduced into system, or failure to pump pedal before moving vehicle. Solution: Pump brake pedal 3-5 times until firm. If still soft, bleed brakes using Honda DOT 3/4 fluid (OEM 08798-9008).
ABS or brake warning light illuminated: Warning light remains on after service. Root cause: Damaged or disconnected pad wear sensor (Hybrid), low brake fluid, or sensor wire pinched during reassembly. Solution: Inspect and reconnect/replace sensor (OEM 45255-TVA-A01), top off fluid, clear codes with scan tool if needed.
Rotor scoring or vibration: Pulsation or vibration during braking. Root cause: Rotor below minimum thickness (0.98 in/25 mm), excessive runout, or improper wheel lug nut torque. Solution: Measure rotor thickness, replace if below spec (OEM 45251-TVA-A01), torque lug nuts to 80 ft-lbs (108 Nm) in star pattern.
