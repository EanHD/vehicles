📋 Step-by-Step Procedure

1Park vehicle on level ground, engage parking brake, and allow engine to cool until warm (not hot). Wear gloves and eye protection.
⏱️ 5 min
2Raise vehicle safely using a jack and support with jack stands. Locate oil drain plug (passenger side, front of oil pan) and oil filter (location varies by engine; consult service manual for exact position).
⏱️ 5 min
3Position drain pan under drain plug. Remove drain plug using correct socket (typically 14mm). Allow oil to drain completely (5-10 minutes). Inspect drain plug washer/gasket; replace if damaged.
⏱️ 10 min
4Remove old oil filter using oil filter wrench. Clean filter mounting surface with a lint-free rag. Ensure old filter gasket is not stuck to engine.
⏱️ 5 min
5Apply a thin film of new oil to the rubber gasket of the new oil filter. Install new filter hand-tight, then tighten an additional 3/4 turn with filter wrench (do not overtighten).
⏱️ 5 min | 🔧 18 ft-lbs
6Install drain plug with new washer (if required). Torque drain plug to specification.
⏱️ 2 min | 🔧 27 ft-lbs
7Lower vehicle. Remove oil fill cap (passenger side valve cover). Add specified quantity of 5W-30 full synthetic oil (quantity varies by engine; typically 6-7 quarts—verify owner’s manual).
⏱️ 5 min
8Replace oil fill cap. Start engine and run for 1-2 minutes. Shut off engine and wait 2 minutes. Check oil level with dipstick; add oil if necessary to bring level between MIN and MAX marks.
⏱️ 5 min
9Inspect for leaks around drain plug and oil filter. Wipe any spilled oil. Reset oil life monitor via instrument cluster menu if equipped.
⏱️ 3 min

🔍 Common Issues & Troubleshooting

Oil leaks from drain plug: Caused by worn, missing, or improperly torqued crush washer. Always replace washer and torque to spec. If threads are damaged, repair with thread insert or replace oil pan.
Stripped oil filter threads: Over-tightening or cross-threading during installation. Hand-tighten filter, then 3/4 turn with wrench. If threads are damaged, replace oil filter adapter.
Incorrect oil viscosity or type: Using non-synthetic or wrong viscosity oil can reduce engine life and performance. Always use 5W-30 full synthetic meeting Ford WSS-M2C961-A1 spec.
Oil life monitor not reset: Failure to reset monitor can lead to incorrect service intervals. Follow reset procedure in owner’s manual.
Oil overfill or underfill: Incorrect oil level can cause engine damage. Always check level with dipstick after refill and after engine run-up.

💡 Pro Tips

💡Replace drain plug crush washer at every oil change to prevent leaks.
💡Hand-tighten oil filter first, then 3/4 turn with wrench—do not overtighten.
💡After refilling, run engine for 1-2 minutes, then recheck oil level and for leaks.
💡Keep a record of oil changes and reset oil life monitor each time.
💡For hybrid models, consult service manual for high-voltage safety procedures.
