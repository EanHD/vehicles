Safety Precautions

Never work under a vehicle supported only by a jack—always use jack stands.
Allow engine to cool slightly before draining oil to avoid burns, but ensure oil is warm for complete drainage.
Wear gloves and eye protection to prevent contact with hot oil and chemicals.
Dispose of used oil and filter properly—do not pour oil on ground or into drains.

Service Procedure

  1. Park vehicle on level ground, engage parking brake, and allow engine to warm up for 3-5 minutes (do not let it get too hot). Wear gloves and eye protection.
    Time: 5 min
  2. Safely raise vehicle using a jack and support with jack stands. Locate oil drain plug and oil filter (refer to owner's manual for exact locations).
    Time: 5 min
  3. Position drain pan beneath oil drain plug. Using a 14mm socket, loosen and remove drain plug. Allow oil to drain completely (5-10 minutes). Inspect drain plug washer/gasket; replace if damaged.
    Time: 10 min
  4. Remove old oil filter using oil filter wrench. Clean oil filter mounting surface thoroughly.
    Time: 5 min
  5. Apply a thin film of new oil to the rubber gasket of the new oil filter. Install new filter by hand until snug, then tighten an additional 3/4 turn with filter wrench. (Torque: 18 ft-lbs)
    Time: 3 min
  6. Reinstall drain plug with new washer. Torque drain plug to specification. (Torque: 27 ft-lbs)
    Time: 2 min
  7. Lower vehicle to ground. Remove oil filler cap and add specified quantity of oil (typically 6-7 quarts for V6, 7-8 quarts for V8; always verify in owner's manual). Use a funnel to avoid spills.
    Time: 5 min
  8. Start engine and let it run for 1-2 minutes. Shut off engine and wait 2 minutes. Check oil level with dipstick; add oil if necessary to bring level between MIN and MAX marks.
    Time: 5 min
  9. Inspect area around drain plug and oil filter for leaks. Wipe away any spilled oil.
    Time: 2 min
  10. Reset oil life monitor if equipped (refer to owner's manual for procedure).
    Time: 2 min

Common Issues & Troubleshooting

Oil leaks from drain plug: Caused by worn, missing, or improperly torqued crush washer. Always replace washer and torque to spec. If threads are damaged, repair with thread insert or replace oil pan.
Stripped oil filter threads: Over-tightening can damage filter housing. Hand-tighten filter, then 3/4 turn with wrench only. If housing is damaged, replacement may be necessary.
Incorrect oil type: Using oil not meeting Ford WSS-M2C961-A1 spec can affect engine performance and warranty. Always use recommended viscosity (typically 5W-30 for most engines).
Oil overfill: Adding too much oil can cause foaming and engine damage. Always check level with dipstick after refill and after engine run-up.
Oil life monitor not reset: Failure to reset monitor can lead to incorrect service intervals. Follow owner's manual procedure to reset after each oil change.
