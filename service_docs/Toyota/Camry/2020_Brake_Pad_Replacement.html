Safety Precautions

Always support vehicle with jack stands at manufacturer lift points; never rely on hydraulic jack alone.
Wear eye protection and gloves to prevent injury from debris and chemicals.
Do not hang caliper by brake hose; use wire or bungee to support.
Compress caliper piston slowly to avoid damaging ABS or EPB systems.
Check brake fluid level before and after compressing pistons to prevent overflow.
Ensure all fasteners are torqued to specification before driving.
Test brakes at low speed before returning vehicle to service.

Service Procedure

  1. Loosen wheel lug nuts (21mm socket) on the brake service axle while vehicle is on the ground. Do not remove yet.
    Time: 5 min
  2. Raise vehicle using a hydraulic jack at manufacturer-specified lift points. Secure with jack stands. Ensure vehicle is stable before proceeding.
    Time: 7 min
  3. Remove wheel lug nuts and wheel. Set aside in a safe location.
    Time: 3 min
  4. Inspect brake assembly for uneven wear, leaks, or damaged components. Note pad thickness and rotor condition.
    Time: 4 min
  5. Remove the two 14mm caliper slide bolts (front/rear). Support caliper with wire or bungee; do not hang by brake hose. (Torque: 25 ft-lbs (front/rear caliper slide bolts))
    Time: 5 min
  6. Remove old brake pads and anti-rattle clips. Note wear pattern for diagnostic purposes.
    Time: 3 min
  7. Remove and clean caliper slide pins. Lubricate with high-temperature brake grease and reinstall.
    Time: 5 min
  8. Compress caliper piston slowly using a brake caliper tool. Monitor brake fluid reservoir to prevent overflow. For rear brakes with electronic parking brake (EPB), activate service mode before compressing piston.
    Time: 7 min
  9. Install new anti-rattle clips (if supplied). Place new brake pads with anti-squeal shims in correct orientation.
    Time: 4 min
  10. Reinstall caliper over new pads. Install and hand-tighten caliper slide bolts. Torque to specification. (Torque: 25 ft-lbs (front/rear caliper slide bolts))
    Time: 5 min
  11. Reinstall wheel. Hand-tighten lug nuts in star pattern. Lower vehicle and torque lug nuts to specification. (Torque: 76 ft-lbs (wheel lug nuts))
    Time: 5 min
  12. Pump brake pedal until firm. Check brake fluid level and top off if needed. For rear EPB, deactivate service mode.
    Time: 5 min
  13. Test drive: Bed-in new pads with 30 gentle stops from 30 mph. Listen for abnormal noises and verify proper braking.
    Time: 15 min

Common Issues & Troubleshooting

Uneven pad wear: Often caused by seized caliper slide pins or improper lubrication. Symptoms include tapered pads or one pad worn more than the other. Solution: Clean and lubricate slide pins every service.
Brake noise (squeal or grinding): Can result from missing or misaligned anti-rattle clips, worn shims, or lack of brake grease. Solution: Ensure all hardware is installed and lubricated per spec.
Soft brake pedal after service: Usually due to air in brake lines or caliper piston not fully seated. Solution: Pump pedal until firm; if issue persists, bleed brakes.
ABS or brake warning lights: May occur if caliper piston is compressed too quickly or with ignition on. Solution: Always compress piston slowly and with ignition off; scan for codes if warning persists.
Rear electronic parking brake (EPB) malfunction: Failing to activate service mode before compressing rear piston can damage EPB actuator. Solution: Always follow EPB service mode procedure before rear pad replacement.
