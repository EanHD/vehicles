üîç Common Issues & Troubleshooting

Electronic Parking Brake (EPB) service mode failure: Caused by not following timing sequence precisely or low battery voltage. Symptoms include EPB not releasing or error messages. Solution: Ensure battery is fully charged (12.5V+), follow 8-second timing window exactly, listen for motor wind-back confirmation sound. If repeated failures occur, use OBD-II scanner to check for EPB system codes.
Brake fluid reservoir overflow during piston compression: Occurs when reservoir is overfilled or piston compressed too quickly. Can cause fluid spill on painted surfaces (corrosive). Solution: Remove some brake fluid with turkey baster before compression, compress piston slowly while monitoring reservoir level, keep clean rags around reservoir cap area.
Seized caliper slider pins: Caused by lack of lubrication, contamination, or torn dust boots allowing moisture intrusion. Symptoms include uneven pad wear, pulling during braking, reduced brake performance. Solution: Remove pins completely, clean with wire brush and brake cleaner, inspect dust boots for tears (replace if damaged), apply generous amount of synthetic brake grease to smooth pin sections only, ensure free movement in bracket.
Premature inner pad wear: Typically caused by seized slider pins preventing caliper float or corroded caliper bracket. One pad wears significantly faster than other. Solution: Thoroughly clean bracket contact surfaces, replace abutment clips, verify slider pins move freely, check caliper for binding. Consider caliper replacement if bracket is severely corroded.
Brake pedal travels to floor after pad replacement: Caused by not pumping pedal to reset piston position or air in brake lines. Solution: Pump brake pedal 10-15 times with engine OFF until firm. If pedal remains soft, bleed brake system starting with wheel furthest from master cylinder. Check for fluid leaks at caliper and brake lines.
Brake noise after replacement (squeal or grinding): Multiple causes including missing or improperly installed anti-squeal shims, pad contact points not lubricated, wrong pad compound, glazed rotors. Solution: Verify shims are attached to all pads, apply brake grease to pad backing plates and abutment clip contact points (never on friction material), resurface or replace glazed rotors, ensure correct OEM-specification pads are installed.
Rotor thickness below minimum specification: Rotors on 2020 Camry with EPB must be minimum 10.5mm (0.414 in). Below this causes pedal pulsation, reduced stopping power, potential brake failure. Solution: Always measure rotor thickness with micrometer at multiple points including thinnest section. Replace rotors if at or below minimum specification - never machine below minimum. Note that non-EPB models have different specification (8.5mm).
