Safety Precautions

Never work under vehicle supported only by hydraulic jack - ALWAYS use jack stands rated for vehicle weight (3+ tons for full-size vehicles)
Use jack on designated lift points only - consult owner's manual for locations to avoid damage to underbody components
Allow engine to cool 15-30 minutes before draining oil to prevent severe burns - fresh oil can exceed 200°F
Wear nitrile gloves and safety glasses when handling used oil - contains carcinogens and heavy metals
Dispose of used oil and filter at certified recycling center - most auto parts stores accept used oil free of charge - do NOT dump in trash, ground, or storm drains

Service Procedure

  1. Raise the vehicle using a floor jack and secure with jack stands at designated lift points per owner's manual. Remove the front undertray by loosening four 10mm bolts.
    Time: 5 min
  2. Locate the oil drain plug (14mm head) on the oil pan. Position a drain pan (minimum 6 qt capacity) under the plug.
    Time: 2 min
  3. Remove the oil drain plug using a 14mm socket and allow oil to drain for 5-10 minutes until flow slows to a drip. Inspect and replace the drain plug washer/gasket (OEM 90430-12031).
    Time: 10 min
  4. Reinstall the drain plug with new washer and torque to 27 ft-lbs (37 Nm) using a torque wrench. (Torque: 27 ft-lbs (37 Nm))
    Time: 2 min
  5. Remove the old oil filter using a 64mm or 65mm oil filter wrench. Clean the filter mounting surface with a rag.
    Time: 3 min
  6. Apply a thin film of fresh engine oil to the new filter gasket. Install new oil filter (OEM 04152-YZZA6) hand-tight plus 3/4 turn after gasket contact. (Torque: 18 ft-lbs (24 Nm) or hand-tight plus 3/4 turn)
    Time: 3 min
  7. Reinstall the undertray and secure with four 10mm bolts. Tighten bolts by hand; do not overtighten.
    Time: 2 min
  8. Lower the vehicle safely to the ground.
    Time: 2 min
  9. Add 4.8 quarts (4.5 liters) of 0W-16 full synthetic engine oil (API SP, ILSAC GF-6A) through the filler neck using a funnel.
    Time: 3 min
  10. Reinstall and tighten the oil filler cap. Start engine and run for 1-2 minutes. Shut off, wait 2-5 minutes, then check oil level on dipstick. Oil should be between MIN and MAX marks.
    Time: 5 min
  11. Inspect for leaks around drain plug and oil filter. Wipe any spilled oil from engine and undertray.
    Time: 3 min
  12. Reset oil maintenance light via steering wheel controls: Settings > Scheduled Maintenance > Reset Data > Yes.
    Time: 2 min

Common Issues & Troubleshooting

Oil leaks from drain plug: Most common cause is worn/reused crush washer. ALWAYS replace the copper/aluminum washer at each service (OEM part ~$1). If threads are damaged from overtightening, consider oversized drain plug (Fumoto valve, HeliCoil insert) or oil pan replacement.
Stripped drain plug threads: Result of overtightening (exceeds 30 ft-lbs) or cross-threading. Use correct socket size (14mm or 17mm), verify torque specification (typically 27-30 ft-lbs for this vehicle), apply in straight motion. Repair options: oversized drain plug, thread repair kit, or oil pan replacement ($150-400 parts + labor).
Incorrect oil viscosity used: This model requires 0W-16 (newer Toyota/Lexus) or 0W-20 (older models). Using 5W-30 or 10W-30 will reduce fuel economy by 1-3% and may affect emissions. Do NOT substitute heavier oils unless specified for severe service. Verify specification on oil cap or owner's manual.
Overfilled oil level: Adding too much oil (>0.5 qt over max mark) causes aeration, foaming, increased crankcase pressure, and potential seal damage. Check level on LEVEL ground with engine at operating temperature, then wait 5-10 minutes for oil to settle before reading dipstick. Drain excess immediately if overfilled.
Oil filter housing leak: Ensure O-ring is properly seated and lubricated with clean oil before installation. Torque canister-style filters to specification (18-22 ft-lbs typical) or hand-tighten cartridge-style 3/4 turn after gasket contact. Double-O-ring (old gasket stuck to housing) causes immediate leak - always inspect housing before installing new filter.
