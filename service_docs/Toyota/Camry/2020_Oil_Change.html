Safety Precautions

Never work under vehicle supported only by jack; always use jack stands.
Allow engine to cool before draining oil to prevent burns.
Wear gloves and eye protection to avoid contact with hot oil and debris.
Dispose of used oil and filter according to local environmental regulations.
Ensure vehicle is in Park (automatic) or gear (manual) with parking brake engaged.

Service Procedure

  1. Gather all required tools, parts, and safety gear. Ensure vehicle is parked on a level surface and engine is warm but not hot.
    Time: 3 min
  2. Raise the front of the vehicle using a jack and secure with jack stands. Remove the plastic under-engine cover by loosening four 10mm bolts.
    Time: 5 min
  3. Position oil drain pan under the oil pan. Remove the oil filler cap and dipstick to allow oil to drain more freely.
    Time: 2 min
  4. Remove the oil drain plug (14mm) and allow oil to drain completely (5-10 minutes). Inspect and replace the drain plug washer. (Torque: 27 ft-lbs)
    Time: 10 min
  5. Remove the oil filter using a 65-67mm oil filter wrench. Clean the mounting surface. Lubricate new filter gasket with fresh oil.
    Time: 5 min
  6. Install new oil filter. Tighten by hand until gasket contacts base, then an additional 3/4 turn (or torque to 18 ft-lbs if using a filter housing). (Torque: 18 ft-lbs)
    Time: 3 min
  7. Reinstall oil drain plug with new washer. Torque to 27 ft-lbs (37 Nm). (Torque: 27 ft-lbs)
    Time: 2 min
  8. Reinstall under-engine cover and secure with four 10mm bolts.
    Time: 3 min
  9. Lower vehicle to ground. Add 4.8 quarts (4.5 liters) of 0W-16 synthetic oil through the oil filler neck. Reinstall oil filler cap.
    Time: 5 min
  10. Start engine and let run for 1-2 minutes. Shut off engine, wait 2 minutes, then check oil level on dipstick. Top off if necessary.
    Time: 4 min
  11. Inspect for leaks at drain plug and oil filter. Reset oil maintenance light via vehicle settings menu.
    Time: 3 min

Common Issues & Troubleshooting

Oil leaks from drain plug: Caused by worn or reused crush washer. Always replace washer at each service. If threads are damaged, use an oversized drain plug or helicoil insert.
Stripped drain plug threads: Results from overtorquing or cross-threading. Use correct 14mm socket and torque to 27 ft-lbs. Severe damage may require oil pan replacement.
Incorrect oil viscosity: Using oil other than 0W-16 (e.g., 0W-20 or 5W-30) can reduce fuel economy and affect engine performance. Always verify correct viscosity in owner's manual.
Oil filter housing overtightened (hybrid): Cartridge filter housing overtightened can crack or deform housing. Always torque to 18 ft-lbs.
Undertray not reinstalled: Skipping undertray reinstallation can expose components to debris and water. Always reinstall securely.
