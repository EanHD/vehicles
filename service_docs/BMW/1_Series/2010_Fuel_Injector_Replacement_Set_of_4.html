Safety Precautions

Always relieve fuel system pressure before disconnecting lines to prevent injury.
Work in a well-ventilated area; avoid sparks or open flames near fuel.
Wear safety glasses and gloves when handling fuel and injectors.
Use proper jack stands if lifting vehicle; never rely solely on a jack.
Disconnect battery before servicing to prevent electrical shorts.

Service Procedure

  1. Disconnect the negative battery terminal to prevent electrical hazards.
    Time: 5 min
  2. Remove the upper engine cover and any additional covers obstructing access to the fuel rail and injectors.
    Time: 10 min
  3. Relieve fuel system pressure by locating the Schrader valve on the fuel rail, covering it with a rag, and pressing the valve to release pressure.
    Time: 5 min
  4. Disconnect the fuel supply line from the fuel rail using a fuel line disconnect tool.
    Time: 5 min
  5. Unplug the electrical connectors from each fuel injector and the fuel rail wiring harness. Carefully move wiring out of the work area.
    Time: 10 min
  6. Remove the four M6 hex bolts securing the fuel rail to the cylinder head. (Torque: 9 Nm (6.6 ft-lbs))
    Time: 5 min
  7. Carefully lift the fuel rail assembly with injectors attached. Wiggle gently to free injectors from their bores. Place rags to catch any spilled fuel.
    Time: 10 min
  8. Remove the metal retaining clips from each injector and pull the injectors from the fuel rail. Inspect and replace all injector seals (O-rings and decouplers).
    Time: 15 min
  9. Install new injectors into the fuel rail, ensuring all seals are properly seated and lubricated with a small amount of engine oil. Secure with new retaining clips.
    Time: 15 min
  10. Carefully lower the fuel rail with new injectors into the cylinder head, ensuring injectors seat fully into their bores.
    Time: 10 min
  11. Reinstall the four M6 fuel rail mounting bolts and torque to specification. (Torque: 9 Nm (6.6 ft-lbs))
    Time: 5 min
  12. Reconnect all electrical connectors to the injectors and fuel rail.
    Time: 5 min
  13. Reconnect the fuel supply line to the fuel rail.
    Time: 5 min
  14. Reinstall all engine covers and components removed for access.
    Time: 10 min
  15. Reconnect the battery. Start the engine and check for leaks. Perform a post-service diagnostic scan and clear any fault codes.
    Time: 10 min

Common Issues & Troubleshooting

Leaking injector seals: Causes rough idle, fuel smell, or misfires. Always replace all seals during injector service.
Stuck or seized injectors: Common in high-mileage vehicles. Use penetrating oil and gentle twisting/pulling. Avoid excessive force to prevent bore damage.
Electrical connector damage: Brittle plastic on older vehicles can crack. Inspect connectors carefully; replace if damaged.
Fuel rail alignment issues: Misaligned rail can cause poor injector seating and leaks. Ensure rail is seated evenly before tightening bolts.
Post-service misfires or rough running: Often due to air in fuel system or incorrect injector coding. Perform adaptation reset and clear fault codes.
