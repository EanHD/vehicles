⚠️ Safety Warnings

⚠️Always disconnect the negative terminal first and reconnect it last to prevent short circuits.
⚠️Wear safety glasses and gloves to protect against acid and electrical shock.
⚠️Do not smoke or create sparks near the battery; batteries emit explosive hydrogen gas.
⚠️Use proper lifting technique; batteries are heavy and can cause injury.
⚠️Dispose of old batteries at an authorized recycling center; do not discard in regular trash.

📋 Step-by-Step Procedure

1Park the vehicle on a flat surface, set the parking brake, turn off the ignition, and remove the key. Open the hood and locate the battery on the passenger side of the engine compartment.
⏱️ 2 min
2Wear safety glasses and gloves. Using a 10mm wrench or socket, loosen and disconnect the negative (black, -) battery cable first. Move the cable away from the battery terminal to prevent accidental contact.
⏱️ 2 min | 🔧 44 in-lbs (battery terminal clamp nut)
3Loosen and disconnect the positive (red, +) battery cable using a 10mm wrench or socket. Move the cable aside.
⏱️ 2 min | 🔧 44 in-lbs (battery terminal clamp nut)
4Remove the battery hold-down clamp using a 13mm socket and ratchet. Set the clamp and bolt aside.
⏱️ 3 min | 🔧 13 ft-lbs (battery hold-down bolt)
5Carefully lift the battery straight up and out of the tray. Use a battery carrying strap if available. Batteries are heavy (approx. 45-55 lbs).
⏱️ 3 min
6Inspect the battery tray for corrosion or debris. Clean with a wire brush and battery cleaner as needed. Clean the cable terminals with a terminal brush.
⏱️ 4 min
7Place the new battery (Group 48/H6 or 94R/H7, as specified) into the tray, ensuring correct orientation of terminals.
⏱️ 2 min
8Reinstall the battery hold-down clamp and tighten the bolt to specification.
⏱️ 2 min | 🔧 13 ft-lbs (battery hold-down bolt)
9Connect the positive (red, +) cable to the battery terminal and tighten the clamp nut to specification.
⏱️ 2 min | 🔧 44 in-lbs (battery terminal clamp nut)
10Connect the negative (black, -) cable to the battery terminal and tighten the clamp nut to specification.
⏱️ 2 min | 🔧 44 in-lbs (battery terminal clamp nut)
11Double-check all connections for tightness. Start the vehicle and verify normal operation. Reset clock, radio presets, and any learned memory functions as needed.

🔍 Common Issues & Troubleshooting

Low voltage codes (e.g., P0562): May appear after battery replacement if system voltage is low during or after installation. Clear codes with scan tool and verify charging system operation. [4]
Loss of memory settings: Radio presets, clock, and seat/mirror memory may be lost during battery replacement. Reprogram as needed after installation.
Corroded terminals: Corrosion can cause poor connection and starting issues. Clean terminals thoroughly before installing new battery.
Improper battery group/size: Installing incorrect battery group (wrong size or terminal orientation) can cause fitment or electrical issues. Always verify correct group (48/H6 or 94R/H7) for engine and trim. [2]
Loose or overtightened terminals: Loose clamps cause intermittent electrical faults; overtightening can damage posts. Always torque to 44 in-lbs.

💡 Pro Tips

💡Use a memory saver device to retain electronic settings if available.
💡Apply dielectric grease to terminals after installation to prevent future corrosion.
💡If vehicle exhibits warning lights or codes after replacement, perform a battery relearn or drive cycle as required by GM procedures.
💡Check for battery registration or reset procedures in the owner's manual for models with advanced battery monitoring systems.
💡If using a charger or maintainer, ensure it is compatible with AGM batteries.
