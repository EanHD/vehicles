Safety Precautions

Always wear safety glasses and gloves when handling batteries—acid is corrosive and can cause injury.
Disconnect the negative terminal first and reconnect it last to prevent accidental short circuits.
Do not smoke or create sparks near the battery—hydrogen gas may be present and is highly explosive.
Use proper lifting technique—batteries are heavy and awkward to handle.
Dispose of old batteries at an authorized recycling center; do not discard in regular trash.

Service Procedure

  1. Park the vehicle on a level surface, set the parking brake, turn off the ignition, and remove the key. Open the hood and secure it with the prop rod.
    Time: 2 min
  2. Locate the battery on the passenger side of the engine compartment. Identify the positive (+, red) and negative (-, black) terminals.
    Time: 1 min
  3. Using a 10mm wrench or socket, loosen the negative (black) terminal clamp bolt. Remove the negative cable and secure it away from the battery. (Torque: 89 in-lbs (7.4 ft-lbs) for terminal clamp bolt)
    Time: 2 min
  4. Loosen the positive (red) terminal clamp bolt with a 10mm wrench or socket. Remove the positive cable and secure it away from the battery. (Torque: 89 in-lbs (7.4 ft-lbs) for terminal clamp bolt)
    Time: 2 min
  5. Remove the battery hold-down clamp using a 13mm socket and ratchet. Set the clamp and bolt aside. (Torque: 13 ft-lbs for hold-down bolt)
    Time: 3 min
  6. Lift the battery straight up and out of the tray. Use a battery carrying strap if available. Batteries are heavy—use proper lifting technique.
    Time: 3 min
  7. Inspect the battery tray for corrosion or debris. Clean the tray and terminal clamps with a wire brush and battery cleaner as needed.
    Time: 3 min
  8. Install the new battery (Group 48/H6 or 94R/H7, AGM recommended) into the tray, ensuring correct orientation of terminals.
    Time: 2 min
  9. Reinstall the battery hold-down clamp and tighten the 13mm bolt to 13 ft-lbs. (Torque: 13 ft-lbs for hold-down bolt)
    Time: 2 min
  10. Connect the positive (red) terminal first. Tighten the 10mm terminal clamp bolt to 89 in-lbs (7.4 ft-lbs). (Torque: 89 in-lbs (7.4 ft-lbs) for terminal clamp bolt)
    Time: 2 min
  11. Connect the negative (black) terminal last. Tighten the 10mm terminal clamp bolt to 89 in-lbs (7.4 ft-lbs). (Torque: 89 in-lbs (7.4 ft-lbs) for terminal clamp bolt)
    Time: 2 min
  12. Verify all connections are secure. Start the vehicle to confirm proper operation. Reset any electronic systems as needed (radio presets, clock, etc.).
    Time: 3 min
  13. Dispose of the old battery at an authorized recycling facility.
    Time: 3 min

Common Issues & Troubleshooting

Battery drain after replacement: May be caused by improper terminal connection or failure to reset vehicle electronics. Confirm all connections are tight and clear any diagnostic codes with a scan tool if warning lights persist.
Corrosion on terminals: White or blue-green buildup can cause poor starting or charging. Clean terminals thoroughly and apply dielectric grease to prevent recurrence.
Vehicle electronics malfunction: Loss of radio presets, clock, or warning lights after battery replacement. Some modules may require re-initialization or a scan tool to clear codes.
Premature battery failure: Caused by incorrect battery group size, low-quality battery, or excessive parasitic draw. Always use OEM-recommended AGM battery and check for abnormal current draw if repeated failures occur.
Hold-down clamp not tightened to spec: Can result in battery movement, vibration damage, or electrical issues. Always torque hold-down bolt to 13 ft-lbs.
